Tacos Gobernador

Description

Tacos Gobernador are a popular Mexican seafood dish from the state of Sinaloa (just like Aguachile). The story goes that Sinaloa’s Gobernor used to request his Shrimp Tacos with cheese and that’s how these tacos got started! You may think that cheese and seafood does not go well together but in this case, everything turns out delicious! Give it a try.

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 medium onion; chopped. Yellow or white
  • 1 medium bell pepper; chopped
  • 1 lb shrimp; peeled and cut into small pieces
  • 2 cup shredded Chihuaha or mozzarella cheese
  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • Extra butter to fry the tacos

Steps

  1. In a large skillet (an iron skillet preferably), add some butter and saute the onions and bell peppers.
  2. Cook until soft for about 2 minutes. Add the shrimp and cook for another 3 to 4 minutes or until the shrimp is no longer translucent. Note: Make sure not to overcook the shrimp as it can become rubbery.
  3. Assemble the tacos by adding a layer of shrimp and top it with shredded cheese.
  4. On a separate skillet, melt some butter with 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Place the tacos open to warm up the entire tortilla.
  5. As soon as the tortilla becomes soft, using a spatula, fold it to cover the shrimp side.
  6. Flip the tacos to brown the other side. Cook until crispy and the cheese has melted. Repeat process until all tacos are done.
